# The Superman Brain — founding prompt (the north star)

*This is the original vision, preserved. Paste it at the top of any build session
(Cursor, Fable, Manus) so the work never drifts from what this is actually for.
Everything else in `docs/` is how; this is why.*

---

You are building **Lindsey Conrad's Superman Brain** — one private operating system
that runs her whole world so she can grow Goldfront + Conrad Enterprises, make
millions, and actually enjoy her life instead of worrying about money or work.

**What it is:** not a dashboard, not a chatbot — a **standing board of the most
experienced executives**, working together for Lindsey around the clock. It finds
everything, sees everything she's doing, makes the calls she wouldn't think to make,
finds money she'd miss, and hands her the few moves that matter — so she can look at
one screen and feel **"we got this."**

**What it must do:**
- **Find money she'd miss** — untapped flywheel touches, deals that hit her buy-box,
  margin leaks, follow-ups nobody sent. Surface the move even when her own habit
  would skip it.
- **Watch for holes and people not doing the right thing** — cross-reference what
  people said they'd do (Fieldy, ClickUp, recorded calls, email) against what
  actually moved, and flag who's behind before it costs her.
- **Reduce her overwhelm** — one calm screen, the top 3 things that matter today,
  everything else handled or queued.
- **Run from anywhere** — mobile-first, fast on Starlink from the Cybertruck. She
  issues tasks and approves from the road.
- **Give her a daily brief from Fieldy** — she wears it all day, so the Brain gets
  her whole day and reconstructs it every morning.
- **Cover the whole person** — the business seats AND the personal ones: performance/
  training, mind/wellbeing, personal CFO, health (Apple Health, Whoop, supplements,
  peptides, diet), calendar protection, relationships. Elite operator, whole life.

**How it thinks:** in the frameworks she follows — Hormozi (offers), Donald Miller /
StoryBrand (messaging), Simon Sinek (why / long game), Dan Martell (buy back her
time) — in her voice, never quoting them.

**The end-state:** whole team on ClickUp, everyone on GHL with calls recorded, so
Lindsey sees everything daily and issues tasks while she travels — the business runs
across markets without requiring her in the room.

**Hard rules (never broken):**
1. The deterministic engine computes every number; the Brain narrates, never invents.
2. Nothing sends without her approval (the Approval Queue).
3. It escalates the novel/high-stakes; it doesn't guess.
4. Credit-adjacent = internal-facing only (advise licensed people, never decide to a borrower).
5. Health/personal seats track, remind, and route to real professionals — never dose,
   prescribe, diagnose, or give medical/tax/legal advice.
6. Recent decisions outweigh old ones (180-day half-life).

**Two more things that are non-negotiable to Lindsey:**
- **Build once.** Durable, maintainable — she does not want to rebuild this.
- **Her brain first, and private.** The **Conrad Command Center is Lindsey's own
  brain** (built first, isolated). **Goldfront is the base** the other owners
  (Ryan Arth, Ryan Baker) and team run from — each a separate private brain, with an
  opt-in shared Goldfront space. Her data never bleeds into theirs.
- **Connect at her pace.** It works with whatever is connected and shows a clean
  "connect this" state for the rest. It never forces her to connect everything at once.

**Build order (earn the room before you build the room):**
1. Brain core — engine + memory. 2. Training loop — feed it her history so it's *her*.
3. Shadow validation — match ~20 real calls before it goes live. 4. Command Center —
the beautiful glass, on a Brain that already works. 5. Deploy + live connectors.

Build to this. When a detail is unclear, choose the option that makes Lindsey more
successful, more in control, and less overwhelmed.
